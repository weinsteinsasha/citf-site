A legendary evening of fairy tales and cabaret with a world-renowned classic
‘A Tale of the Clock' and Pani Lyusya Cabaret is a landmark event honoring the work of Lyudmila Petrushevskaya, widely regarded as the most influential woman in contemporary Russian literature. Across two acts, the performance reveals the vivid talent, uncompromising resilience, and paradoxical humour that define a celebrated classic.
The first act features the play "A Tale of the Clock". Directed by internationally aсclaimed artist Fyodor Pavlov-Andreevich, this play explores the deep duality of women in a touching story where daughters test mothers and love triumphs over death. The production marks a historic one-time only theatrical debut for Natalia Sindeeva, founder of independent media TV Rain. She is joined on stage by co-star Evgenia Borzykh, a leading actress and frontwoman of the band SBPCH.
The second act transforms the evening into a spirited Pani Lyusya Cabaret. The atmosphere shifts to a "hooligan-style" musical celebration, where Lyudmila Petrushevskaya herself performs world-famous hits featuring her own brilliant, newly reimagined lyrics. This intimate, chamber experience serves as a moving tribute to the human spirit, combining high drama with the rebellious joy of a cabaret legend.
Duration: 1h 45 min (with intermission)

The Artist
Lyudmila Petrushevskaya is a titan of culture, recognized as one of the most translated and celebrated Russian-speaking authors living today. A novelist, playwright, singer, and artist, her work has been described by The New Yorker as a cornerstone of "contemporary world literature".

She is joined by a powerhouse creative team, including director Fyodor Pavlov-Andreevich, an internationally acclaimed artist and protégé of Marina Abramovic. The cast features Evgenia Borzykh, a leading actress and frontwoman of the band SBPCH, and media icon Natalya Sindeeva, founder of the independent channel TV Rain.
The Festival
This performance is part of The Cyprus International Theatre Festival, running from March 2 to March 8, 2026. CITF brings world-class performers from across the globe to Cyprus. This season, the festival stage is dedicated to the power of the individual female voice under the theme "SHE IS HERE." The festival invites you to the Pattihio Theatre in Limassol to experience a week of performances and events that celebrate the strength, truth, and presence of women who stand on their own.
